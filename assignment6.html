A6: Molds and Casting 2
Originally, I planned to do a mold of latinum, but after printing the mold it became appearent that it would be too thin to cast well. It was also kind of a boring choice to cast since it could easily just be printed. I still liked the idea of doing a prop though, so I browsed thingiverse for inspiration. There I found this horga'hn design, also from Star Trek. This one seemed like an excellent choice - not just a more interesting 3D design, but something difficult to print. It needed to be printed in pieces and then glued together, and also required supports. Conveniently someone else had even made a variation of the design to be printable without supports, though it was also in two pieces - but these pieces were split right down the middle, so it was perfect for a mold!

Painting the gold was a bit too messy, going to see if applying thin strips would work better. But horga'hns don't always have gold bands so it's ok.
Overall the horga'hns were a success! You can see the progress left to right (first made to last) of improving casting techinque to remove the tiny air bubbles, though the larger ones still remain. But still good enough for jamaharon.
